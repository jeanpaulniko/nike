What This Is

NÍKĒ is a free, uncensorable, deniable messaging system. It uses steganography — hiding messages inside normal-looking text — so that even if your communication is monitored, the monitor sees nothing suspicious.

Three channels:

Channel 1 — Zero-Width Unicode: Hides data in invisible characters between letters. Fast. Invisible to human eyes. Stripped by aggressive text scrapers.

Channel 2 — Whitespace: Encodes data in patterns of spaces and tabs. Survives most filters. Fails if text is reformatted.

Channel 3 — Linguistic: Encodes data in word choice — synonyms, sentence structure, punctuation patterns. Survives everything: DPI, AI scrapers, printing and retyping, translation. This is the channel for high-threat environments.

Deniability: Under duress, enter a different passphrase — you get different (innocent) output. Mathematically impossible to prove another message exists.
